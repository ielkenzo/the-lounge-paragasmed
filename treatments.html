Care tailored to the concern

The information below is educational and does not replace diagnosis. Options, preparation, downtime, cost and expected outcomes are confirmed during consultation.

01
Scalp & follicle care

Hair loss treatment

Hair shedding and thinning can have hormonal, nutritional, inflammatory, genetic or medication-related causes. Assessment may include scalp examination, history review, laboratory requests and close monitoring before treatment is selected.

Plans may include

  • Medical treatment for suitable patterns of hair loss
  • Scalp care and regenerative procedures when appropriate
  • Progress photography and response-based plan adjustments
Best first stepScalp consultationCourseUsually stagedResultsGradual and variable
02
Clearer, healthier skin

Acne treatment

Acne care is built around the type and severity of breakouts, sensitivity, pigmentation risk and the presence of scarring. The goal is to control active disease first, then address marks and texture safely.

Plans may include

  • Prescription or clinic-directed topical and oral care
  • Comedone management, peels or device-assisted treatments
  • Scar and post-acne pigmentation planning after control
AssessmentAcne type & triggersFollow-upUsually requiredPriorityPrevent new scars
03
Medical dermatology

Pathologic skin cases

Rashes, infections, changing lesions and persistent symptoms need diagnosis—not cosmetic guesswork. A dermatologist reviews the history and examines the skin before recommending tests, medication, monitoring or a procedure.

Common consultations

  • Dermatitis, psoriasis, fungal and bacterial conditions
  • Moles, cysts, warts and other skin growths
  • Persistent itching, discoloration or unexplained changes
Urgent signsRapid or painful changeApproachDiagnosis firstScopeAll ages
04
Energy-based dermatology

Laser resurfacing & more

Laser and light treatments can address selected concerns involving texture, scars, pores, pigment, redness and unwanted hair. Device, wavelength and settings are chosen for the diagnosis and skin type—not simply the trend.

Possible treatment goals

  • Resurfacing for acne scars, fine lines and rough texture
  • Selected pigment and vascular concerns
  • Laser hair reduction and combination treatment plans
PreparationVaries by deviceDowntimeMinimal to several daysEssentialSun protection
05
Expression-line treatment

Botox® & neuromodulators

Botulinum toxin treatment temporarily relaxes selected muscles. Careful facial assessment, conservative dosing and precise placement help preserve natural expression while addressing appropriate lines or medical indications.

Consultation covers

  • Facial movement, symmetry and treatment priorities
  • Medical history, contraindications and aftercare
  • Expected onset, duration and review schedule
ProcedureOften 15–30 minutesOnsetGradualDurationIndividual
06
Skin quality protocols

Skin boosters

Skin boosters are injectable or procedure-assisted treatments selected to support hydration, texture and overall skin quality. Products differ substantially, so formulation, authenticity and suitability should be discussed with the physician.

Options may include

  • Hyaluronic-acid hydration protocols
  • PN/PDRN-based regenerative treatment where appropriate
  • Combination plans with energy-based procedures
ProductVerified and disclosedCourseMay need sessionsDowntimeSmall bumps or bruising
07
In-clinic procedures

Minor surgical procedures

Selected skin lesions can be managed through a planned office procedure after examination. The method depends on the diagnosis, location, size, scarring risk and whether tissue should be sent for laboratory analysis.

May include

  • Biopsy or removal of suitable moles, cysts and benign growths
  • Wart procedures and selected nail or wound care
  • Local anesthesia, sterile technique and scheduled follow-up
